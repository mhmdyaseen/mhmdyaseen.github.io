The Great Mosque of Mecca.

Masjid al-Haram also known as the Great Mosque of Meccais a mosque that surrounds the Kaaba in Mecca, in the Makkah Province of Arabiat is a site of pilgrimage in the Hajj, which every Muslim must do at least once in their lives if able, and is also the main phase for the ʿUmrah, the lesser pilgrimage that can be undertaken any time of the year. The rites of both pilgrimages include circumambulating the Kaaba within the mosque. The Great Mosque includes other important significant sites, including the Black Stone, the Zamzam Well, Maqam Ibrahim, and the hills of Safa and Marwa.
